Spectral Effects

Spectral effects directly augment the frequency profile of a guitar audio signal. By emphasizing the mid-frequencies, a guitar solo can stand out in the mix. By adding addition harmonic frequencies, a guitar tone can sound fuller. The most popular spectral effect for guitar is the overdrive pedal, which pushes the amplitude of the guitar's frequency signature up to and past the point of distortion. Variations of overdrive and distortion create the sound of blues and rock guitar.

Equalizer (EQ)

EQ allows a guitarist to change the character of their guitar sound by boosting or cutting the level of various frequencies or bands. Another popular use for EQ is to cut the frequencies that are causing feedback and prevent those frequencies from feeding back through the guitar amplifier.

Boss Equalizer GE-7 Pedal

The Boss Equalizer GE-7 is a 7 band graphic equalizer with effect level control. This EQ stompbox provides sliders to control 7 octave bands from 100Hz to 6.4kHz by 15dB up or down.

User Controls

Graphic Equalizer Sliders: Allows guitarist to boost or cut frequencies in the following bands: 100Hz, 200Hz, 400Hz, 800Hz, 1.6kHz, 3.2kHz, and 6.4kHz.

Level Slider: This slider sets the output level of the equalized signal.


Wah-Wah

A Wah-Wah pedal is a foot-controlled bandpass filter that is applied to your guitar signal. The pedal action is similar to an accelerator pedal of a car. As you rock the Wah footpedal, the resonant peak frequency is swept across the frequency bands from 350Hz to 2.5kHz. The net effect is a "waaah" tone. Jimi Hendrix's signature sound is from the combination of a Cry Baby Wah-Wah pedal and a Big Muff distortion pedal.

Line 6 Fassel Wah Model

The Line 6 Fassel Wah model is an emulation of the original Dunlop Cry Baby Super Wah Wah pedal. The photo of the Fassel wah model is only showing the display portion of the Helix floor processor. Click or tap on the photo to enlarge.

User Controls

Foot Pedal Position: Controls the tone of the guitar sound. By rocking the pedal up and down, you can create the popular "Waaaah" sound.

FcLow: Sets the bottom end of the wah frequency spread.

FcHigh: Sets the high end of the wah frequency spread.

Mix: Controls the amount of the effect signal that is mixed in with the guitar signal.

Level: Controls the level output of the pedal.


Overdrive

Overdrive is a kind of soft or transparent distortion where the guitar's signal is raised towards the maximum level accomodated by the overdrive circuit. The result is raspy guitar tone which is popular in blues and rock guitar music.

Boss OverDrive OD-1X Pedal

The Boss OverDrive OD-1X is an overdrive guitar pedal that can make your guitar tone sound like you are playing through an overdriven guitar amplifier, but at lower volume settings. The effect is like playing through an amp with a ripped speaker cone.

User Controls

Level: Adjusts the volume of the overdrive effect.

Low: Adjusts the low frequency tone of the effect.

High: Adjusts the high frequency tone of the effect.

Drive: Adjusts the amount of overdrive.


Distortion

Distortion effects can make your guitar sound like it's playing through an aggressively overdriven guitar amplifier without the need to play at high volume levels. The distortion effect is similar, but much more pronounced than an overdrive pedal.

Boss Distortion DS-1X Pedal

Boss Distortion DS-1X is a popular distortion pedal with great sustain and consistent tone.

User Controls

Level: Adjusts the volume of the overdrive effect.

Low: Adjusts the low frequency tone of the effect.

High: Adjusts the high frequency tone of the effect.

Dist: Adjusts the amount of distortion.
