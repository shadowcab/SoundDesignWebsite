Time-Based Effects

Modulation

Modulation effects involve one signal controlling or varying some parameterized characteristic on the guitar's audio signal. This process is called modulation. Modulation can make a guitar sound very surreal.

Boss Super Chorus CH-1 Pedal

The Boss Super Chorus passes the guitar signal through a series of short delays that are modulated and mixed together to create a slight pitch variation. The net affect is a fatter, pulsating sound that sounds like multiple players are performing.

User Controls

E.Level: The effects level sets the mixing level of the effect sound, which is a version of the original guitar signal with a varying pitch.

EQ: This is a tone control for the effects signal.

Rate: This sets the rate of the "pulsating" chorus effect.

Depth: This sets the depth or pitch shift of the chorus effect.


Delay

A delay effect creates a controllable echo effect by sampling the guitar signal and repeating it back a set number of times.

Boss Digital Delay DD-7

The Boss Digital Delay is a compact stereo delay that offers modulation, analog, reverse, and "hold" delay modes.

User Controls

E.Level (Effect Level): Controls the level of the effect sound.

F.Back (Feedback): Controls the number of repeats.

D.Time (Delay Time): Controls the delay time between repeats.

Mode: Sets the delay mode. Ordinary delay includes delay times from 1ms to 3200ms. Special delay modes include Hold, Modulate, Analog, and Reverse.


Reverb

Reverb effects create a variety of player-selected room reverberations for a guitar signal. The idea is to create the reverb ambiance of various spaces for your guitar sound.

Line 6 Legacy Hall Reverb

Line 6 Hall Reverb allows the user to configure seven reverb parameters: decay, predelay, low cut, high cut, mix, level, and trails(on/off). The reverb ambiance is that of a large hall space. The photo of the Line 6 Hall Reverb is only showing the display portion of the Helix floor processor. Click or tap on the photo to enlarge.

User Controls

Decay: The amount of time for the reverb tail to decay away. The longer the delay, the large the larger the room ambiance will feel.

Predelay: The amount of time before the reverb effect is mixed with the original guitar signal. This can help prevent your guitar sound from becoming muddy or too washed out.

Low Cut: Controls the amount of low frequencies in the effect signal. This can help prevent your guitar sound from becoming muddy or too washed out.

High Cut: Controls the amount of high frequencies in the effect signal. This can add brighness or shimmer to the reverb tail.

Mix: Controls the amount of reverb effect mixed in with the guitar signal.

Level: Controls the level of the reverb effect.

Trails: This enables or disables the reverb trails on your guitar signal.
