Types of Effects

According to Sweetwater Sound (Time-Based Effect | Sweetwater, 2002), guitar effects fall into one of three effect types: Dynamic, Spectral, or Time-Based. Dynamic refers to those effects that affect the loudness or level of your guitar’s audio signal. Spectral has to do with augmenting the frequency profile of an audio signal and Time-Based effects involve changing some feature of an audio signal temporally.

Although, Sweetwater Sound does not dive deeper or refine the definition of these categories, I think it's obvious that all effects have an affect on the dynamics and spectra of an audio signal and some effects process all three aspects with an impact on dynamics, spectra, and time. This means that the lines between these categories is blurred and they should be considered very basic and broad. In other words, many effects exhibit the characteristics of more than one category.

Dynamic Effects

Dynamic effects are effects that affect the loudness or level of your guitar’s audio signal. Volume pedals and compressors fall into this category.

Learn more about Dynamic effects here.

Spectral Effects

Spectral effects makeup the largest category of effects. It's comprised of any effect that augments the frequency profile of an audio signal. This category includes all Overdrive effects, Distortion effects (which is just a more aggressive overdrive), EQ effects, other filter effects, Wah Wah effects, and Pitch and Synth effects.

Learn more about Spectral effects here.

Time-Based Effects

Time-based effects are effects that change some feature of an audio signal temporally. Modulation, delay/echo, and reverb effects fall into this category.
