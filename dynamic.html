Dynamic Effects

Compressors

A compressor reduces the guitar's dynamic range. Dynamic range is the difference in dBs from the softest note to the loudest note played. By reducing the dynamic range, the volume level of harsh and peaky notes is automatically reduced and the level of soft, quiet notes is raised. The compression process will also make quiet notes sustain longer. The net affect is a tighter, pleasing sound. But too much compression can squash the sound, making the performance sound lifeless and dull.

MXR CSP202 Custom Comp Compressor Pedal

The MXR Custom Comp has three external controls (Output knob, Sensitivity knob, and footswitch) and two internal controls (Attack knob and Trim knob) that are located inside the case. It also features True Bypass so the effect doesn't affect your signal when turned off.

User Controls

Output: Controls the output level.

Sensitivity: Controls the amount of compression up to 36 dB.

Attack: Only accessible from inside the device. Controls the reaction speed of the compressor before reducing the level. This allows the attack of the notes be heard before compressing.

Trim: Only accessible from inside the device. This is a high-end treble control.


Noise Gates

A noise gate is a dynamics device that removes unwanted low-level noises from your guitar audio signal that fall below a user-set threshold level. Potential noise sources are single-coil guitar pickups and 60Hz hum from nearby power cables.

Line 6 Helix Noise Gate

The Line 6 Noise Gate is a built-in, Line 6 Helix effects block. It can be configured for mono or stereo operation and provides controls for threshold, decay, and level. The photo of the noise gate is only showing the display portion of the Helix floor processor.

User Controls

Threshold: Sets the threshold level where the unwanted noise is gated off.

Decay: Sets the delay in milliseconds from the time the threshold is triggered and the time that audio is gated off. This allows the threshold to be set lower so soft notes can be heard and to prevent quiet notes from getting abruptly cut off.

Level: This sets the output level of the noise gate.
